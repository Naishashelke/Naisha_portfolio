Research Work

Skin Cancer Classification using SAVE Imaging

Conducted advanced research at National Chung Cheng University on AI-driven skin cancer diagnosis using Snapshot Assisted Visual Enhancement (SAVE) hyperspectral imaging technology.

Developed and evaluated five hybrid machine learning models to classify melanoma and related skin lesion types. The research focused on improving diagnostic performance through feature optimization and comparative model evaluation using accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score metrics.

This work contributes to the development of intelligent healthcare systems that support early-stage cancer detection using artificial intelligence. The research paper based on this work is currently under review.

Breast Cancer Detection using Deep Learning

Conducted research during undergraduate studies at Presidency University focusing on medical image classification using deep learning models.

Designed and implemented a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model to classify breast cancer images for early diagnosis.

The study aimed to improve diagnostic reliability using artificial intelligence techniques applied to medical imaging datasets. This research work was published in the International Journal of Scientific Research and Engineering Development (IJSRED).

International Research Internship – Taiwan Experience Education Program (TEEP)

Participated in an international research internship at National Chung Cheng University in Taiwan under the Taiwan Experience Education Program.

Worked on machine learning models applied to medical imaging datasets for skin cancer detection. The research involved dataset analysis, model training, feature optimization, and performance evaluation.

The project received an "EXCELLENT" performance rating for research contribution and technical implementation.
